01 / The concept

Not a boba shop.
A whole kitchen.

People think we're a tea spot with snacks. We're a Taiwanese kitchen — bentos, noodle soups, sizzling tappan plates, table-sized hot pots, night market classics — that also happens to make really good boba.

The menu's deep on purpose. Bring your parents, bring a date, bring six friends and a hot pot. We've fed all of them.

"It's the only place in Cerritos where I can order stinky tofu and a strawberry frosty in the same breath."

03 / The kitchen

Eight ways
to order us.

The menu's long because Taiwanese food is wide. Here's the cheat sheet — pick a corner, dive in.

01

Night market plates

Popcorn chicken, stinky tofu, oyster omelet, calamari. Order three, share everything.

02

Bento boxes

Pork chop, popcorn chicken, salmon. Comes with rice, egg, pickles, vegetables. Lunch sorted.

03

Noodle soups

Hand-pulled noodles, hours-long broths. Beef, wonton, seafood, pork bone. Cold-day fuel.

04

Sizzling tappan

Cast-iron plates that hit the table still sputtering. Beef, garlic chicken, peppercorn fish. A whole show.

05

Hot pot for the table

Bubbling broth, your friends, two hours of dipping. Spicy, satay, miso, tomato. Group order, every time.

06

Milk teas & boba

302 Milk Tea, taro, honey, jasmine, Hokkaido caramel. Add egg pudding, grass jelly, honey boba. Stack it deep.

07

Frosties & slushies

Blended fruit slushies, creamy frosties, frozen lattes. The summer side of the menu. Always on.

08

Shaved snow

Mountain of fluffy snow, condensed milk, real fruit, mochi, pudding, boba. Dessert built for sharing — or not.
